The School of the Future

    By 2017, the educational institution had almost fully taken shape – closely resembling the vision talented Odessa architects had designed back in 2012. Finishing work was in full swing in the spacious classrooms, sports hall, swimming pools, and concert hall. For the spiritual development of future students, another temple—St. John the Theologian's Church—was built, located below the Temple of St. Sergius of Radonezh. Notably, in April 2017, the first baptisms were held in the upper temple.

    The unique educational complex was increasingly referred to as the "School of the Future". By August 2018, it had reached a new level of material infrastructure. The task set by Serhii Kivalov for the contractors was successfully completed – students, teachers, and all staff had to feel comfortable in the school year-round. A comprehensive list of advanced technologies was installed, including solar panels, heat pumps, full ventilation systems, and a modern boiler room. Next to the complex, a powerful heliosystem with 100 solar collectors was installed, ensuring the school remained warm, bright, and cozy—and most importantly, safe.

    Strict fire safety standards were followed – alarms and 24/7 video surveillance were installed. Teachers underwent specialized training to work at the School of the Future. Each classroom was equipped with interactive panels and the latest technology, allowing teachers to integrate classical subjects with Christian ethics, chess, swimming, and robotics—an unparalleled educational approach in Ukraine.

The Long-Awaited Opening

    In September 2020, the school officially opened its doors to students. According to Serhii Kivalov, then president of the International Academic School Odessa, although the school was designed for 1,250 students, it was planned to initially enroll no more than 200. As expected, the School of the Future launched with 100 students in preschool and primary grades. As the institution grew, it expanded its programs and services to include middle and high school levels.

    A year later, in September 2021, in addition to the indoor sports hall, the school's material infrastructure was enhanced with an outdoor sports complex featuring a football stadium with grandstands for hundreds of spectators, athletic tracks, and a modern gym.

Innovative Education

    Beyond classical subjects, the modern curriculum introduced mandatory disciplines, including swimming, chess, Christian ethics, and robotics. From first grade, students study foreign languages. Alongside the typical English, French, and German languages, Hebrew was also added. Special emphasis is placed on STEM subjects: biology, mathematics, physics, and chemistry. Physical education is also a priority, so in addition to required subjects, children can choose from various sports and creative activities – football, wrestling, acrobatics, dance, music, theater, visual arts, and engineering design.

    The school boasts a highly qualified faculty, which includes educators from Ukraine, the USA, and other countries. These teachers bring international school experience and are committed to helping students achieve success, including university admissions both in Ukraine and abroad.

Minister of Education's Visit to the School of the Future

    In July 2021, the Minister of Education and Science of Ukraine, Serhii Shkarlet, visited the International Academic School Odessa, touring various facilities. "This is a model school, an example to follow. It seems to me that if every person with resources built at least one such institution in their region, the level of education in our country would improve significantly," the minister remarked.

    Serhii Shkarlet was particularly impressed by the innovations implemented at the School of the Future. He was especially pleased with the robotics and programming courses, the diverse dietary menu for students, and the powerful sports and wellness facilities built to European standards, as well as the interactive boards installed in every classroom without exception.

    Since September 2021, the "Easy Language Exchange" foreign language program has been launched. Through this program, students can develop their foreign language communication skills by exchanging ideas with native speakers of the language they are studying. In the new academic year, international educators eagerly prepare interactive speaking exercises for students to engage them in interesting conversation-based learning.

    Today, the International Academic School "Odessa" is one of the largest and most prestigious educational institutions not only in Ukraine but also in Europe. As of the 2023-2024 academic year, the school has over 700 students from Ukraine and around the world. MASHO remains a center of educational and sports life in Southern Ukraine: the school's cinema and concert hall hosts the city's most significant events; while its sports complex hosts national and international competitions in chess, gymnastics, swimming, football, and many other sports. Of course, such a rich variety of events has an extremely positive impact on the well-rounded development of students at the International Academic School "Odessa".

The year 2024 – Elections for the first-ever student parliament of the International Academic School Odesa

    On March 21, 2024, the International Academic School Odessa marked a historic milestone—the election of its first-ever School Parliament, the primary student self-governance body. Students from grades 5 to 10 actively participated, nominating candidates and voting for representatives from each grade level. In total, 14 parliament members were elected, including the Head of the Student Parliament.

    In their first addresses, the newly elected representatives shared ambitious plans, such as creating a music center, a new chemistry lab, improving the school uniform, and founding a volleyball team. This significant step marks just the beginning of an exciting journey in student governance, aimed at enhancing the learning environment and engaging students in shaping their school's future.
