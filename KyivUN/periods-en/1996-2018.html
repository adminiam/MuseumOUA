Establishment of the Educational Institution

    The history of establishing and developing the Kyiv Institute of Intellectual Property and Law of the National University "Odesa Law Academy" began in 1996, when, following a joint decision of the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine Commission on Science and Education and the Ministry of Ukraine for Science, Technology, and Industrial Policy the CJSC "Institute of Intellectual Property and Law" was founded.

    The main focus of the newly established educational institution was training highly qualified specialists for the state system of intellectual property legal protection and enterprises, organizations, and institutions of all ownership forms; specialized improvement of education and professional training in intellectual property, scientific, technical, and innovative activities.

Training the Best Specialists in Intellectual Property Law

    On November 27, 2004, by order of the Ministry of Education and Science of Ukraine No. 821, the institute was designated as the lead institution for training, retraining, and professional development in intellectual property. In 2008, following the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine directive from October 10, 2007, and the Ministry of Education and Science order from November 29, 2007, the CJSC "Institute of Intellectual Property and Law" was transformed into the State Higher Educational Institution "State Institute of Intellectual Property."

    Since then, the Kyiv Institute of Intellectual Property and Law has gained full status as a capital structural division of the Odesa Law Academy. However, compared to other University branches, Kyiv developed "by leaps and bounds" in both educational and material-technical aspects.

     During the period from 2011 to 2016, 1,546 people received specialist and master's degrees. The high quality of specialist training at the institute is evidenced by the following facts: at that time, out of 414 patent attorneys in Ukraine, about 150 were institute graduates; patent attorney agencies (patent law firms) are headed by institute graduates I. O. Spasky, D. E. Benatov, V. V. Badanin, I. V. Kondratyuk, and others.

The Best Educational Center of NU "OLA"

    Therefore, in November 2014, to comply with the Law of Ukraine "On Higher Education," by order of the rector of the National University "Odesa Law Academy," the institute was renamed to the State Institute of Intellectual Property of the National University "Odesa Law Academy" in Kyiv, thus securing a special, nationally significant status.

    Such remarkable scientific and educational potential of Kyiv University naturally facilitated the development of its material and technical base: the construction of educational buildings, dormitory, recreational zones for students, and sports complexes.

Development of Material and Technical Base

    In 2017, the comprehensive renovation of the dormitory of the Kyiv Institute of Intellectual Property and Law of the National University "Odesa Law Academy" was completed. Besides modern rooms of European style, a large gym was built at the dormitory where students can maintain their physical fitness free of charge. They can also professionally engage in gymnastics and martial arts: boxing, karate, judo, pankration, etc.

    The modern infrastructure of the dormitory was noted by student-athletes of the Kyiv Institute – candidates for master of sports, winners of international and all-Ukrainian competitions, as well as the president of the National University "Odesa Law Academy" Serhiy Kivalov, who was present at the opening.

Development of Recreational Zones for Students

    On September 18, 2018, projects for improving the Student Campus territory were implemented. One such project was the opening of the "Carpathian Waterfall" complex. The best landscape designers and builders from western Ukraine worked on the project, creating a true wonder corner where a powerful waterfall stands beautifully among neat greenery and stones, combined with unique walkways that skillfully cut through flower lawns.

    As Kyiv journalists wrote that day: "Trees are planted around to protect from city noise and dust. The mini-park and complex are equipped with everything necessary for pleasant leisure time: sufficient cast benches, urns, and street lamps are installed. Students constantly visit here, and residents of the Darnytskyi district of Kyiv, looking at the picturesque beauty constantly being created on the institute's territory, are happy that the Kyiv Institute of Intellectual Property and Law NU OLA has become not only a scientific educational center but also a true decoration of the capital's Left Bank."

Construction of Mini-Park "Carpathian Hill"

    It should be noted that shortly before this, on September 1, 2017, another recreational mini-park "Carpathian Hill" was opened, a wonder corner where rocky hills combine with unique walkways that skillfully cut through flower lawns. The mini-park "Carpathian Hill" is literally filled with small green islands where even during unbearable heat, one can hide in the shade of trees and enjoy communion with nature. At the opening of the student mini-park, besides University leadership and representatives of state authorities, famous TV host and showman Dmytro Komarov was present.

Sports Life - An Integral Part of Student Development

    Continuing the sports and wellness development of students, in 2018, construction began on an extremely powerful and large-scale project - a sports and wellness complex, which would include a large professional football field, excellent spectator stands, spacious changing rooms, fitness and gym facilities.

    On September 18, 2018, the laying of the first stone - the capsule of the sports complex of the Kyiv Institute of Intellectual Property and Law of the National University "Odesa Law Academy" took place. The first stone laid in construction passes a kind of relay to future generations who will honor university traditions and worthily carry the title of KUIPL NU "OLA" student.

    At the ceremonial event of starting the sports complex construction, present were: President of the National University "Odesa Law Academy" Serhiy Kivalov; Deputy Minister of Education and Science of Ukraine Roman Hreba; Head of Kyiv City State Administration Vitaliy Klychko and Rector of the Kyiv Institute Vasyl Felyk.

International Cooperation and Social Orientation

    Besides infrastructural achievements, the Kyiv Institute of Intellectual Property and Law expands cooperation with state and international institutions, particularly in patent justice. Thus, on October 4, 2017, a Memorandum of Cooperation was signed between the National University "Odesa Law Academy" and the "Patent and Trademark Office" of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

    The public law entity, represented by Acting Director General Gunel Sevdimaliyeva and the President of the National University "Odesa Law Academy," Academician Serhiy Kivalov, signed a Memorandum of Cooperation aimed at: acquiring professional skills in chosen specialties by University students and staff development of the "Patent and Trademark Office" of the Republic of Azerbaijan; implementing joint projects, programs, and events, including symposiums, conferences, seminars, round tables, and many others.
