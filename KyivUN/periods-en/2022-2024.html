October 2022. University Rector Vasyl Felyk Donates Funds Raised at a Charity Fair to a Unit of the Armed Forces of Ukraine

    From the very first days of the full-scale invasion, the teams of the National University "Odesa Law Academy," the International Humanitarian University, and the Kyiv University of Intellectual Property and Law, under the leadership of Serhiy Kivalov, united their efforts and immediately began providing comprehensive support to the Armed Forces of Ukraine, the State Border Guard Service, Territorial Defense Units, and other military formations defending the territory of Ukraine.

    Over the course of two years of war, students and faculty members of these universities have conducted hundreds of charity events in support of soldiers, donated blood for wounded defenders almost every month, allocated part of their salaries to meet the needs of the Armed Forces of Ukraine, and delivered tens of thousands of rations, equipment, and material resources.

KIIVP – A Record Holder in Supporting Defenders

    While the National University "Odesa Law Academy" distinguished itself primarily by high rates of blood donations for wounded defenders and financial support, the Kyiv University of Intellectual Property and Law took the lead from the beginning of the war in providing material and technical assistance to the defenders, particularly by producing camouflage nets for military personnel. The University's Student Council, the trade union committee, and the entire faculty, and all other university staff have actively participated in the production of these nets.

    A milestone in this effort was the establishment of a National Record of Ukraine on September 21, 2023, by the team of the Kyiv University of Intellectual Property and Law for creating the "Largest Map of Ukraine Made of Camouflage Netting." This record was set on September 21, the International Day of Peace in Ukraine. The event was attended not only by the University's leadership but also by the First Deputy Head of the Kyiv City Military Administration, Major General of the Armed Forces of Ukraine Volodymyr Kydon, who especially praised the patriotic education provided to KIIVP students. The largest camouflage net in Ukraine was handed over to support the defenders of the Armed Forces of Ukraine.

KIIVP Students Deliver Clothing and Equipment for the Armed Forces of Ukraine

    Kyiv University of Intellectual Property and Law Actively Engaged in Volunteer Activities. On June 15, 2023, at the initiative of the University President, Academician Serhiy Kivalov, and with the support of University Rector Vasyl Felyk, equipment, gear, and clothing were provided to the servicemen of the 4th Mechanized Battalion of the Separate Presidential Brigade named after Hetman Bohdan Khmelnytskyi.

    Present at the delivery of material aid, Deputy Commander of the 4th Mechanized Battalion of the Presidential Brigade, Colonel Oleh Mukan, expressed his gratitude to the leadership, academic staff, and students of KIIVP for their ongoing support of the battalion, including the purchase of a drone that is critically important for military personnel to carry out combat missions and bring Ukraine closer to victory.

Educational Cooperation

    Despite the conditions of a full-scale war, the University, adhering to the best traditions of the Odesa Law Academy, continued to actively engage in educational and scientific activities. In February 2023, the Kyiv University of Intellectual Property and Law signed a memorandum with the Cyber Police Department of the National Police of Ukraine, represented by its head, Police Colonel Yuriy Vykhodets. This partnership has significantly enhanced the practical training of KIIVP students specializing in Cybersecurity (123), Law (081), and Law Enforcement (262).

    The main areas of cooperation include: Enhancing the level of professional training for police officers, public servants, and academic staff; Incorporating advanced practical experience in combating cybercrime into the educational process; Preparing joint scientific events (conferences, round tables, training sessions), as well as scientific works, monographs, and manuals on combating cybercrime.

Collaboration with the Heart Institute of the Ministry of Health of Ukraine

    During the period of 2022–2023, key cooperation agreements were also signed with the Heart Institute of the Ministry of Health of Ukraine represented by the world-renowned cardiac surgeon, Academician Havrylo Kovtun, aimed at training highly qualified specialists in the specialty of "Nursing"; with Polyclinic No. 4 of the Dnipro District of Kyiv; with the State Scientific and Technical Library, These partnerships have significantly expanded the KIIVP's literary resources and provided University students with access to a wide range of electronic libraries and registers of the State Scientific and Technical Library.

Holding of the General Assembly of the National Academy of Legal Sciences of Ukraine (NALSU)

  Considering the steadily growing scientific and educational potential of the Kyiv University of Intellectual Property and Law, on June 9, 2023, the institution was chosen by the National Academy of Legal Sciences of Ukraine as the venue for the General Assembly of Academicians. The General Assembly of NALSU was attended by 38 academicians, 71 corresponding members, and 56 delegates from scientific institutions of NALSU. The event, being highly significant in the academic community, was visited by the Head of the Southern Regional Scientific Center of NALSU, Serhiy Kivalov, Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada, Ruslan Stefanchuk, Member of Parliament and Professor Yuliia Hryshyna, Vasyl Kremen, Serhiy Pyrozhkov.

    During this assembly, fateful decisions for the development of legal science in Ukraine were made, particularly regarding the election of Volodymyr Zhuravl as President of NALSU, and the revocation of Viktor Medvedchuk's status as a full member (academician) of the National Academy of Legal Sciences of Ukraine.

The Most Ceremonious Knowledge Day in the History of the University

    Given the well-developed scientific activities of KIIVP and its extensive system of educational and practical cooperation, Knowledge Day 2023 at the Kyiv University of Intellectual Property and Law was celebrated with special solemnity. The university's thousands-strong community was congratulated on the occasion by its President, Academician Serhiy Kivalov.

    It is worth noting that, in honor of Knowledge Day, KIIVP students received congratulations from the Deputy Minister of Education and Science of Ukraine, Andriy Vitrenko; the Head of the Cassation Administrative Court, Mykhailo Smokovych, Member of Parliament of Ukraine Oleksandr Kachura, the Head of the National Agency for Higher Education Quality Assurance, Andriy Butenko; the Head of the State Service of Education Quality, Ruslan Hurak, the Head of the Darnytskyi District State Administration in Kyiv Mykola Kalashnyk, the Head of the Darnytskyi District Court of Kyiv Tetyana Shchasna, and the Head of the Department of Organ and Tissue Transplantation of the Heart Institute of the Ministry of Health of Ukraine Havrylo Kovtun.

All-Ukrainian Chess Tournament

    In the fall of 2023, the First All-Ukrainian Chess Tournament for Preschoolers was held at the Kyiv University of Intellectual Property and Law with the support of the Chess Federation of Ukraine. The tournament brought together preschool-aged children from all over Ukraine. The young chess players demonstrated their incredible skills, sportsmanship, intellect, perseverance, and determination to win. All participants of the tournament received commemorative prizes and gifts.

    On the occasion of the first national-level chess tournament, the event was attended by Volodymyr Voronov, Head of the Subcommittee on Early Development and Preschool Education of the Verkhovna Rada Committee on Education, Science, and Innovation; Vadym Huttsait, President of the National Olympic Committee and Olympic champion - Vadym Huttsait, President of the Kyiv City Rugby Federation - Oleksandr Potapenko, Vice-Rector of the Kyiv University of Culture and Arts - Inna Kostyria, and President of the Boxing Federation of Ukraine - Kyrylo Shevchenko. Additionally, 80,000 square meters of camouflage netting were donated for the needs of defenders.
